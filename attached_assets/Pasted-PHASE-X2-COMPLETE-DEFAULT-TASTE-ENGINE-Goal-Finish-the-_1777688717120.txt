PART 1 — HARD COMEDY VALIDATION

Add validation that rejects ideas if:

- no clear contradiction
- no tension between intention and reality
- reads like an observation instead of a joke
- safe / soft / generic tone
- no specific behavior
- hook is funny but scenario does not express the joke
- scenario is visual but hook does not match it

Required comedy signals:
Each idea must have at least 2 of:
- self-betrayal
- intention_vs_action
- confident_vs_collapse
- denial_vs_reality
- absurd escalation
- self-as-other / relationship metaphor
- identity exposure
- specific embarrassing behavior

Reject if it is merely “relatable” without comedy.

---

PART 2 — ALIGNMENT GUARANTEE

Before returning an idea, verify:

premise → hook → whatToShow → howToFilm

All four must express the same comedic idea.

Reject if:
- hook says one thing but whatToShow shows another
- whatToShow is generic and does not act out the premise
- howToFilm does not match the action
- premise is not visible in the scene

Example:
Premise:
“I treat my to-do list like someone I’m avoiding.”

Hook:
“my to-do list and i are not speaking”

whatToShow:
Open list → stare → close it → put phone face-down

howToFilm:
Phone POV, hold pause, close app, hard cut

That is aligned.

Bad:
Hook:
“my to-do list and i are not speaking”

whatToShow:
Person lying in bed scrolling randomly

That is mismatch.

PART 4 — ANTI-COPY GUARD FROM CURATED HOOKS

Use the curated hooks as taste seed, not copy source.

Create lightweight fingerprints from curated hooks:

- normalized hook text
- major noun pair
- premise skeleton
- mechanism
- tensionType

Reject generated hook if:
- same phrasing
- same sentence skeleton
- same metaphor with different noun
- near-duplicate of curated hook

Allowed:
same ComedyDNA, new execution.

Example:
Curated:
“i ghosted my own to-do list”

Reject:
“i ghosted my own goals”

Allow:
“my goals are waiting for a reply from me”
