====================================================
1. PRODUCT VISION
====================================================

Lumina is a mobile app for short-form creators.

Its purpose is to help creators stop staring at a blank screen and start filming.

The product generates personalized, funny, filmable short-form video ideas that match the user’s taste, humor, comfort level, and filming situation.

Lumina should not feel like:
- a generic AI prompt wrapper
- ChatGPT inside an app
- a content idea generator that gives vague ideas
- a tool that produces “valid” but weird or unfilmable outputs

Lumina should feel like:
“This app understands my humor and gives me something I can actually film right now.”

The core product promise:

A user opens Lumina, answers a few taste questions, and gets 3 strong ideas. At least one should feel funny, personal, low-effort, and immediately filmable.

Finished-product standard:

Every idea should answer:
1. What is the hook?
2. What exactly should the creator show?
3. How should they film it?
4. Why is it funny or relatable?
5. Why does it fit this user?
6. How can they film it with low embarrassment?
7. What is the caption?
8. Can they film it today without thinking too hard?

If a creator cannot immediately understand what to film, the idea fails.

====================================================
2. TARGET USER
====================================================

Target users are short-form creators on TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts.

They may be:
- beginner creators
- inconsistent creators
- micro-creators
- people who want to post but feel stuck
- people who have humor/taste but struggle turning it into ideas
- people who feel awkward filming
- people who need low-effort ideas they can shoot quickly

Key user pain points:
- “I don’t know what to post.”
- “My ideas feel generic.”
- “I don’t know how to turn this idea into a video.”
- “I feel awkward filming.”
- “I don’t want to show my face.”
- “I don’t want to talk.”
- “I need something easy today.”
- “ChatGPT gives too many ideas but I still don’t know what to film.”
- “I want ideas that sound like me.”

Lumina must reduce decision fatigue and filming hesitation.

====================================================
3. CORE PRODUCT LOOP
====================================================

The desired app flow:

1. User signs in.
2. User completes Quick Tune calibration.
3. Lumina generates 3 personalized ideas.
4. User sees idea cards.
5. Each card has:
   - hook
   - caption
   - confidence labels
   - primary “Film This Now” CTA
6. User taps Film This Now.
7. Film-This-Now screen gives practical filming instructions:
   - setup
   - hook / overlay
   - action
   - twist / reveal
   - payoff
   - caption
   - time estimate
   - difficulty
   - no-face/no-voice/text-overlay adaptations
8. User can create/review/export.
9. User actions write signals back:
   - selected
   - exported
   - skipped
   - feedback
   - make another
   - regenerated
10. Future ideas get more personalized and less repetitive.

The habit loop should become:

Open Lumina → get Daily 3 → pick one → Film This Now → export → Lumina learns.

====================================================
7. CURRENT CRITICAL PROBLEM
====================================================

The main blocker is visible content quality.

Hooks have improved, but scenarios and Film-This-Now instructions are still sometimes bad.

The app has produced outputs like:
- “abandoning the fork is my only hobby still”
- “abandon the fork once, slow”
- “Land the contradiction by abandoned the fork”
- “ghosted the calendar knowingly”
- “camera holds as i ghost the calendar knowingly”
- “i faked my own tab”
- “spiral the lockscreen once, slow”
- “WHY does the alarm keep abandoning itself”
- “pick up the gym”
- “overthink the highlighter”
- “dodge the inbox”
- “Set the inbox down where the camera can see it”
- “Lean into the panic beat — the reveal lands here”
- “let the props carry the deadpan”

These are unacceptable.

They make Lumina feel like generic AI and will destroy trust.

The issue is NOT just UI formatting.
The issue is scenario authoring quality.

The generator is still allowed to produce mechanically valid but human-bad scenarios.

The core failure pattern:
strong hook → noun-swapped template → weird action/object phrase → scenario technically passes → user sees nonsense.

Examples:
- inbox should be an email/laptop scene, not an object you set down or dodge
- gym should be shoes/app/door/couch, not something you pick up
- highlighter should highlight notes, not “overthink itself”
- alarm should ring/snooze/blanket, not be abandoned
- calendar should show plan/regret/rain-check, not random unrelated object
- no-face/no-voice should create real alternate filming instructions, not awkward phrase swaps

====================================================
8. FINISHED PRODUCT QUALITY BAR
====================================================

Every generated idea must pass this human-quality test:

Can a real creator immediately understand:
1. what to film,
2. where to film it,
3. what object/screen/action is involved,
4. what the twist is,
5. what the final payoff is,
6. why the hook matches the scenario,
7. how to film it with no face/no voice if needed?

A “valid” scenario is not enough.
It must be human-filmable.

Do not accept:
- impossible actions
- generic verb-object nonsense
- vague emotional beats
- placeholder direction
- unrelated hook/scenario pairing
- weak noun swaps
- unnatural sentence grammar
- comfort adaptations that sound awkward
- Film-This-Now instructions that do not tell the user what to physically do

Good output should sound like:

Hook:
“the alarm itself is the entire pattern”

Good scenario:
Show your phone alarm going off. Your hand hits snooze. Cut to the alarm going off again. This time your hand slowly slides the phone under the pillow while it keeps buzzing.

Good Film-This-Now:
Setup: Place the phone on your nightstand or bed.
0–2s Hook: Put the hook as text overlay.
2–6s Action: Alarm rings. Your hand hits snooze.
6–12s Twist: Alarm rings again. Your hand pauses like it has been personally attacked.
12–18s Payoff: Slide the phone under the pillow while it keeps buzzing.
Caption: “the alarm won. i’m pretending it didn’t.”

Bad output:
“Static wide of the alarm. Step in, pick the alarm up, put it back.”

====================================================
14. FINAL PRODUCT DEFINITION
====================================================

The finished Lumina beta should do this:

A new user answers quick taste questions.
The app gives 3 ideas.
The top idea is funny, clear, personal, and filmable.
The user taps Film This Now.
The app tells them exactly what to show, what to do, what to overlay, and how to finish the beat.
If they do not want to show face or talk, the app adapts the instructions.
If they refresh, they get truly different ideas.
If they select/export/skip, Lumina learns.
When they return, ideas get more personalized without becoming repetitive.

The final product is not a hook generator.

It is a personalized filming-action engine for short-form creators.
