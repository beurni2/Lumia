NEXT UX UPGRADE — Add Clips Guidance + Preview Confidence

Goal:
Reduce thinking during clip capture/upload and make the preview screen push users toward posting.

Do NOT change ideator engine, memory, or generation logic.
Do NOT add publishing, scheduling, analytics, or editor complexity.

---

PART 1 — ADD CLIPS SCREEN: GUIDE WITHOUT THINKING

Problem:
Users may not know what clips to add, especially for 2-clip ideas.

Goal:
Make the Add Clips screen feel obvious and low-effort.

UI:

Title:
“Add your clip”

Subtitle:
“1–2 quick clips is enough.”

Clip slots:

Clip 1
Helper:
“Start with the main moment.”

Clip 2 (optional)
Helper:
“Add a reaction or second angle.”

Important:
Keep labels simple:
- Clip 1
- Clip 2 (optional)

Do NOT use:
- setup
- payoff
- before
- after
- structure language

---

PART 2 — ACTIONS

Each clip slot should offer:

- Film
- Upload

Behavior:
- Film opens camera
- Upload opens picker
- Max 2 clips
- Preserve order
- If user adds only Clip 1, allow continue

CTA:
“Continue”

Disable only if no clip is added.

---

PART 3 — MICRO-CONFIDENCE COPY

Add tiny reassurance copy near continue:

“Don’t overthink it — quick and messy works.”

Goal:
Remove perfection pressure.

---

PART 4 — PREVIEW SCREEN: PUSH TO POST

Problem:
Preview should not feel like a passive mock screen.
It should make the user feel:
“This is ready enough to post.”

Rename / adjust screen tone.

Title:
“Ready to post?”

Subtitle:
“Your video already has the hook, clip, and caption.”

Primary CTA:
“Export video”

Secondary CTA:
“Make another version”

Optional small copy:
“Post it manually wherever you usually post.”

---

PART 5 — CAPTION CONFIDENCE

Show suggested caption clearly.

Label:
“Suggested caption”

Under caption, add:
“Short, casual, and made to match the idea.”

If caption is editable already, keep it.
If not editable, do NOT add editing now unless trivial.

---

PART 6 — REMOVE MOCK FEEL

Avoid language that makes the preview feel fake:
- “template preview”
- “mock”
- “placeholder”
- “demo”

Use language like:
- “preview”
- “ready”
- “export”

---

PART 7 — SUCCESS SCREEN COPY

After export:

Title:
“Video ready”

Subtitle:
“Save it, post it manually, or make another version.”

Buttons:
- “Save to gallery”
- “Make another version”
- “Back to ideas”

Do NOT add posting integration.

---

SUCCESS CRITERIA

User should feel:
1. “I know exactly what clip to add.”
2. “This does not need to be perfect.”
3. “This is ready enough to post.”
4. “I can export and post manually.”

Do NOT add:
- full editor
- trimming
- posting
- scheduling
- analytics
- dashboards
