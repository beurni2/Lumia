PHASE 6E — PREMISE COMEDY SCORING + REJECTION

Goal:
Improve premise quality, not premise quantity.

Phase 6D made premise hooks dominant.
Now we need to ensure the selected premise hooks are actually funny, simple, and post-worthy.

Do NOT change Phase 5.
Do NOT expand the catalog.
Do NOT add new premise styles.
Do NOT chase premise-share higher.
This phase is about quality control.

---

1. ADD PremiseComedyScore

Score each premise hook from 0–10.

Dimensions:

Surprise: 0–2
- unexpected phrasing
- not obvious
- not plain observation

Specificity: 0–2
- tied to the scenario
- not generic
- has concrete behavior/object/emotion

Emotional Punch: 0–2
- self-roast
- embarrassment
- frustration
- anxiety
- delusion
- contradiction

Simplicity: 0–2
- instantly understandable
- short
- one clear joke

Relatability: 0–2
- feels like something people actually do/think
- likely to trigger “me” response

Total = 10

---

2. PASS / FAIL THRESHOLD

Keep:
- score ≥ 7

Rewrite or demote:
- score 5–6

Reject:
- score < 5

Do NOT let weak premise hooks ship just because premise share target exists.

---

3. HARD REJECTION RULES

Reject premise hook if:

- too literal
- not funny
- generic
- too clever/confusing
- reads like a caption instead of a hook
- over-explains the joke
- loses the scenario
- feels like AI wordplay
- has no emotional spike
- could apply to almost anything

Examples to reject:
- "i tried to be productive and failed"
- "this happens every time"
- "i am dealing with the task again"
- "this is relatable"
- "today was difficult"

---

4. BOOST STRONG COMEDY MECHANISMS

Boost hooks that use:

- self-roast
- absurd metaphor
- object personification
- sharp contrast
- identity contradiction
- overdramatic framing
- specific embarrassing behavior
- late-night spiral
- social anxiety
- consumer/phone behavior

Examples:
- "i ghosted my own to-do list"
- "my brain filed for emotional bankruptcy"
- "the fridge knows i’m lying"
- "2am me is a visionary. 9am me is useless"
- "i specialize in disappointing my future self"

---

5. OUTPUT LINE OPTIMIZATION

After scoring, optimize final hook line:

Rules:
- prefer 6–10 words
- max 12 words
- one joke only
- remove filler
- preserve slang/lowercase/fragments
- do not professionalize
- do not make it more complex

Bad:
"i am currently struggling to complete my planned tasks"

Good:
"i ghosted my own to-do list"

---

6. PREMISE VS LEGACY

Premise hooks should still dominate, but quality wins.

If a premise hook scores low and a legacy hook is clearly stronger:
- allow legacy hook to ship

Do NOT sacrifice visible quality just to preserve premise percentage.

---

7. LLAMA RULE

Llama may polish only after a premise hook exists.

Allowed:
- shorten
- make punchier
- improve natural phrasing

Forbidden:
- change premise meaning
- add props/people/locations
- make it formal
- rewrite into generic AI voice

Llama output must be rescored with PremiseComedyScore before accepting.

---

8. QA

Run 10 real batches.

Report:
- premise share
- average PremiseComedyScore
- % hooks scoring ≥7
- top 10 hooks
- bottom 10 hooks
- rejected premise count
- legacy fallback count
- estimated post-worthy rate

Success criteria:
- premise share remains roughly 75–90%
- average PremiseComedyScore ≥7
- broken/boring premise hooks reduced
- post-worthy estimate improves
- no Phase 5 changes
