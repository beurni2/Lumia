That distinction matters.

hook

A bad anchor may not ship, but it can reduce yield, cause repetitive fallback, or create weird “technically valid but culturally off” ideas.

Risk:

weak or stereotyped anchors
anchor works in hook but not in scenario
validators reject too much
region batch becomes repetitive

Fix:

hand-vet anchors
ship small anchor sets
region-by-region rollout
require 20-idea live QA per region
The structural protections are real

I agree these protections are valuable:

Protection	Why it helps
Validators unchanged	Prevents quality floor from dropping
Recipe loop retries	Can recover from bad individual candidates
Claude fallback	Prevents empty batch
Western as control	Gives a stable baseline
Rollback via config	Reduces deployment risk

But they do not fully protect against cultural cringe.

A sentence can pass validators and still feel fake to a Nigerian, Indian, or Filipino creator.

That is the risk your current system cannot automatically detect yet.
