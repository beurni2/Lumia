UX3.1 is not accepted as complete.

The obvious malformed phrases are improved, but live QA still shows the core product problem:
scenarios are mechanically valid but not human-filmable enough.

Examples that still fail human QA:

1. Hook: "my own inbox knows i'm faking it again"
Scenario: "Set the inbox down where the camera can see it... dodge the inbox..."
Problem: You do not set down/dodge an inbox. This should be an email/laptop avoidance scene.

2. Hook: "my own junk is exposing me back!!"
Scenario: "Static wide of the junk... expose the junk..."
Problem: Generic noun-swap. No concrete human action.

3. Hook: "WHY does the highlighter keep overthinking itself"
Scenario: "overthink the highlighter"
Problem: Highlighter does not overthink. The human should over-highlight notes/textbook.

4. Hook: "the gym itself isn't the problem. i am."
Scenario: "pick the gym up, put it back"
Problem: Impossible physical action.

5. Hook: "the alarm itself is the entire pattern"
Scenario: "pick the alarm up, put it back... snooze the alarm"
Problem: Too generic. Should be a snooze/blanket/phone scene.

6. Film-This-Now beat:
"Lean into the panic beat — the reveal lands here."
Problem: Not a filming instruction. It is placeholder language.

7. No-face adaptation:
"let the props carry the deadpan"
Problem: Awkward and not actionable.

Conclusion:
UX3.1 cleaned phrases but did not solve scenario authoring. We need UX3.2 — Authored Scenario Planner.

Goal:
Replace generic object+verb scenario templates with authored, domain-specific scenario plans for common anchors/domains.

Hard rules:
- No migrations.
- No API breaking changes.
- No validator loosening.
- Preserve UX3 refresh/excludeHooks.
- Preserve Hero/Retention/Calibration scoring.
- Keep tests and TypeScript passing.
- Do not only add more validators. Fix the authoring strategy.

Core requirement:
For high-frequency anchors/domains, generate scenarios from domain-authored plans, not generic "object in frame + verb object" templates.

Build authored scenario planners for at least these domains:

1. Inbox/email/work messages
Good scene:
Open inbox or fake inbox. Show unread count. Start replying, delete the reply, switch tabs, come back, mark nothing as read.
Payoff:
Close laptop halfway, then reopen it because avoidance failed.

2. Alarm/sleep denial
Good scene:
Phone alarm rings. Hand snoozes. Alarm rings again. Hand slides phone under pillow/blanket while it keeps buzzing.
Payoff:
Blanket slowly covers phone.

3. Calendar/plans/regret
Good scene:
Open calendar or fake group chat plan. Show commitment from yesterday. Present-self freezes. Type "rain check?" then hesitate.
Payoff:
Pull blanket higher or close calendar.

4. Fridge/food denial
Good scene:
Open fridge. Look at leftovers/vegetables. Close fridge. Immediately open delivery app or grab snack.
Payoff:
Close fridge with respect like it won.

5. Highlighter/study/school
Good scene:
Open notes/textbook. Highlight one sentence. Then another. Then the whole paragraph/page. Understand less.
Payoff:
Put highlighter down like it betrayed you.

6. Gym/fitness avoidance
Good scene:
Put on gym shoes or open fitness app. Move toward door. Sit down to check phone. Still sitting there later.
Payoff:
Shoes stay on, workout never starts.

7. Browser tab/fake productivity
Good scene:
Open laptop with work tab. Open another tab. Then another. Suddenly 12 tabs and no task completed.
Payoff:
Close one tab proudly like progress happened.

8. Profile/story-view mistake
Good scene:
Open fake/demo profile/story. Accidentally tap or view something. Freeze. Close app too late.
Payoff:
Put phone down like evidence.

9. Junk/room clutter
Good scene:
Camera shows clutter pile. Move one item. Reveal more clutter underneath. Put item back like the pile has structural integrity.
Payoff:
Walk away and call it "organized."

10. Mirror/confidence betrayal
Good scene:
Walk into frame confident. Catch reflection. Pause. Lean closer. Immediately adjust lighting/hair or leave frame.
Payoff:
Turn off light or back away like the mirror got personal.

Implementation:
- Create a scenario planner that maps anchor/domain/topicLane to authored scenario plan.
- Prefer authored scenario plan when anchor/domain matches.
- Only fall back to generic templates if no authored family exists.
- Generic fallback must not use impossible physical actions.
- Remove/avoid "pick up {non-physical domain}" for domains like gym, inbox, calendar, thread, profile.
- Remove abstract beats like "Lean into the panic beat" and replace with concrete action.
- Replace no-face/no-voice adaptations with actual alternate instructions, not phrase swaps.

Scenario quality floor:
Every shipped scenario must answer:
1. What object/screen/place is shown?
2. What physical action happens?
3. What changes or gets revealed?
4. What is the final visual payoff?
5. Why does this match the hook?

Reject/repair if any answer is unclear.
