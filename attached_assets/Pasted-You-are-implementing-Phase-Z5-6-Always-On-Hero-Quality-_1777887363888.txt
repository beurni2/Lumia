   - Applies only when user signal is low or absent.
   - Stronger for first generation, cold-start, demo/no-history, low-feedback users.
   - Gradually yields to personalization once user behavior exists.

2. Preserve existing rankers and gates.

Do not loosen hook validators.
Do not reduce anti-copy sensitivity.
The hero score should be an additive overlay that helps choose the strongest already-valid candidates.

3. Improve top-card quality.
The first displayed idea should usually be the strongest combination of:
- hook punch
- filmability
- payoff clarity
- voice fit
- scenario coherence
- safety
- freshness

Do not let a technically valid but plain idea outrank a funnier, more filmable idea.

Penalize top-card candidates with:
- vague hooks
- generic AI-speak
- abstract concepts
- unclear payoff
- hook/scene mismatch
- high-lift setup
- private-data-heavy scene
- repeated skeletons
- weak caption synergy

Examples of vague/generic hooks to penalize:
- “this is where my life collapsed”
- “the moment everything changed”
- “things got crazy”
- “i can’t believe this happened”
- “my life is chaos”
- “this went wrong fast”

4. Add adaptive first-session/cold-start boost.
When user history is low or absent, apply stronger weighting toward broad launch-safe lanes:
- POV/relatable skits
- Surprise twist endings
- Self-deprecating escalation
- Chaotic confession
- Dry deadpan
- Quiet realization

Keep these available but capped:
- overdramatic_reframe
- high_energy_rant

High-energy rant should be rare unless the user explicitly selected it or has signals supporting it.

Cold-start boost should favor:
- broad relatability
- obvious comedic payoff
- low-effort filming
- safe everyday topics
- short hook
- clear physical action
- no private-data dependency

5. Make the boost adaptive.
As user signal increases, personalization should gain weight.

Signals that should reduce first-session boost strength:
- exported ideas
- selected ideas
- likes
- dislikes
- skips
- make-another
- taste calibration
- prior batch history
- ideator_signal history

Returning users should not be forced into generic launch-safe lanes if their behavior shows a different preference.

Principle:
Global hero quality is always-on.
First-session broad-safe bias fades as personalization confidence grows.

6. Batch composition guidance.
For a 3-idea batch, prefer:
- 1 hero idea: strongest funny/filmable/top-card candidate
- 1 taste-aligned idea: closest to creator’s known calibration/signals
- 1 exploratory idea: different but still valid, safe, and filmable

Preserve existing diversity guards.

7. Improve payoff scoring.
Reward ideas where:
- ordinary expectation flips
- object/app betrays user
- creator contradicts themselves
- small action escalates
- physical action is obvious
- caption sharpens the joke
- scenario creates a real comedic event, not just a reaction

Penalize ideas where:
- the hook sounds decent but the scenario has no comedic turn
- the payoff is only a generic reaction
- the scene is just “person reacts”
- the caption does not add anything
- the joke depends on private data
- the idea is too high-lift or requires multiple actors/crowds/special locations

8. Safety for first-session/top-card ideas.
Do not promote top-card ideas that rely on:
- real bank balances
- real private messages
- real medical info
- addresses, IDs, license plates
- salary/confidential work data
- dangerous or unsafe situations
- workplace-confidential details
- real private documents

Private-data-adjacent ideas should either be:
- clearly framed with fake/demo/cropped screens, or
- deprioritized for first-session/top-card placement.
