PHASE 6F — STRICT PREMISESTYLE INTEGRATION (A+ VERSION)

Goal:
Make PremiseStyle the primary comedic engine while ensuring legacy hooks are high-quality fallback, not weak output.

We are NOT removing legacy.
We are upgrading legacy to meet the same quality standard.

---

CORE RULE

PremiseStyle is the ONLY layer that defines the main joke.

Legacy is:
- fallback
- simple hooks
- must still be good

No hook should feel like a leftover or template filler.

---

PART 1 — SELECTION POLICY

Keep current 6E behavior:

- Premise hooks evaluated first (best-scored)
- Legacy used when:
  - no premise ≥5
  - premise pool exhausted
  - legacy clearly better than weak premise

Do NOT force premise share.
Quality > quota.

---

PART 2 — ADD LEGACY COMEDY SCORING

Create:

scoreLegacyComedyScore (0–10)

This is a LIGHTER version of PremiseComedyScore.

---

LEGACY SCORING DIMENSIONS

Relatability (0–3)
- common behavior
- everyday situation
- “this is me” feeling

Clarity (0–3)
- instantly understandable
- no confusion
- no clever phrasing

Simplicity (0–2)
- short
- clean
- one idea

Emotional Signal (0–2)
- mild frustration
- avoidance
- procrastination
- subtle self-awareness

TOTAL = 10

---

PART 3 — LEGACY PASS / FAIL

Keep:
- score ≥ 6

Demote:
- score = 5

Reject:
- score < 5

Reason:
Legacy is simpler, so threshold is slightly lower than premise.

---

PART 4 — HARD REJECT (LEGACY)

Reject if:

- generic filler:
  "this is happening again"
  "today was hard"
  "this always happens"

- too vague:
  applies to anything

- awkward phrasing

- too long (>12 words)

- reads like caption, not hook

- no clear behavior/action

---

PART 5 — ALLOW STRONG SIMPLE HOOKS

Legacy should win when:

- simple but sharp
- behavior-based
- relatable

Examples:

✓ "i opened it. then closed it"
✓ "i said i'll do it later"
✓ "still avoiding it"
✓ "i checked. that counted"
✓ "not today. again"

These should score 6–8 and be allowed to win.

---

PART 6 — PREMISE VS LEGACY DECISION

If both valid:

- premise ≥7 → prefer premise
- legacy ≥7 AND premise ≤6 → allow legacy to win
- both strong → prefer premise (tie bias)

---

PART 7 — DO NOT OVER-REJECT LEGACY

Important:

Do NOT:
- make legacy too strict
- eliminate all legacy hooks
- require legacy to be “clever”

Legacy should feel:
- natural
- human
- simple

---

PART 8 — OUTPUT LINE RULES (BOTH)

- 6–10 words preferred
- max 12 words
- one idea
- conversational
- no over-explaining
- no AI tone

---

PART 9 — LLAMA RULE (UNCHANGED)

- only polish
- preserve meaning
- rescore after mutation

---

PART 10 — QA

Run 10 batches.

Report:

- premise share
- legacy share
- avg PremiseComedyScore
- avg LegacyComedyScore
- % hooks ≥7 (both types)
- top 10 hooks
- bottom 10 hooks
- post-worthy estimate

---

SUCCESS CRITERIA

- no weak legacy hooks
- legacy feels simple but good
- premise still dominates naturally
- output feels consistently post-worthy
- no drop in overall quality

---

IMPORTANT

We are not optimizing for:
- maximum premise share

We are optimizing for:
- maximum quality output

Legacy exists to protect quality, not reduce it.
