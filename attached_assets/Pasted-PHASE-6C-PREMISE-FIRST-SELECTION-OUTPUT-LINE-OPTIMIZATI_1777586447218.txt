PHASE 6C — PREMISE-FIRST SELECTION + OUTPUT LINE OPTIMIZATION

Goal:
Make PremiseStyle hooks the default output because they are clearly better than legacy hook templates.

Target:
80–90% of shipped hooks should come from PremiseStyle.

---

1. PREMISE-FIRST SELECTION

Change selection priority:

Current:
premise hooks are additive / optional

New:
premise hooks are preferred by default

Rules:
- If a valid PremiseStyle hook exists, prefer it
- Legacy hook templates become fallback
- Legacy templates only ship when:
  - no premise hook passes validation
  - premise hook is too long
  - premise hook is unclear
  - premise hook is not funny
  - premise hook conflicts with scenario/safety

Target:
80–90% premise-based hooks across real batches.

Do NOT force 100%.
Keep 10–20% legacy/simple hooks for rhythm and variety.

---

2. OUTPUT LINE OPTIMIZATION

Optimize final hook line before shipping.

The output line must be:

- short
- simple
- funny
- instantly understandable
- conversational
- not over-explained
- not too clever

Preferred:
6–10 words

Hard cap:
12 words max unless existing schema requires lower.

---

3. OUTPUT LINE CHECKS

Reject or rewrite if hook:

- feels like a caption, not a hook
- sounds like a template
- has too many clauses
- is too abstract
- is confusing
- loses the joke
- feels AI-written
- is not funny within 1 second

---

4. COMEDY COMPRESSION

Before final output:

Compress hook by removing:
- filler words
- setup explanation
- unnecessary adjectives
- softeners
- repeated meaning

Example:

Bad:
"i am currently trying to be productive but failing"
Good:
"productivity filed a complaint"

Bad:
"i keep disappointing the goals i made for myself"
Good:
"i specialize in disappointing my future self"

---

5. LEGACY TEMPLATE ROLE

Legacy templates remain available but should be lower priority.

Use them for:
- ultra-simple fragments
- clear relatable moments
- cases where premise hooks fail

Do NOT let legacy templates dominate again.

---

6. DIVERSITY RULES

Within a batch:
- no duplicate PremiseStyle
- no duplicate premise family if possible
- keep HookIntent diversity intact
- keep ideaCoreType diversity intact

Across recent batches:
- penalize reused PremiseStyle
- penalize reused output-line skeleton
- boost unused premise styles

---

7. LLAMA RULE

Llama may polish only after PremiseStyle output exists.

Allowed:
- make hook shorter
- make hook more natural
- preserve slang/fragments/lowercase

Forbidden:
- change premise
- professionalize
- add new scene/object/person
- make joke more complex

---

8. QA

Run 10 real batches.

Report:
- premise hook share
- legacy hook share
- average word count
- top 10 shipped hooks
- worst 10 shipped hooks
- post-worthy estimate

Success:
- 80–90% premise hooks
- average hook length 6–10 words
- fewer boring literal hooks
- no broken grammar
- output feels funnier and simpler
