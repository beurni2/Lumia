Evaluate each idea on this scorecard:

1. Hook strength, 1–5
- 5 = scroll-stopping, specific, emotionally loaded, natural
- 4 = strong, minor polish needed
- 3 = usable but plain
- 2 = generic or predictable
- 1 = reject

2. Filmability, 1–5
- 5 = can be filmed today in under 30 minutes with bedroom/kitchen/desk/car/phone setup
- 4 = easy, minor setup
- 3 = filmable but not immediate
- 2 = requires too much setup/acting/props
- 1 = not realistically filmable

3. Funny / relatable payoff, 1–5
- 5 = genuinely funny or painfully relatable
- 4 = clear comedic tension
- 3 = mildly relatable
- 2 = weak payoff
- 1 = no payoff

4. Freshness / non-repetition, 1–5
- 5 = does not feel like same pattern with new skin
- 4 = somewhat fresh
- 3 = acceptable
- 2 = repetitive
- 1 = obvious duplicate pattern

5. Voice fit, 1–5
- 5 = matches creator/taste/voice strongly
- 4 = mostly aligned
- 3 = neutral
- 2 = mismatched tone
- 1 = wrong voice

6. Safety / privacy, pass/fail
Fail if the idea asks the user to show:
- real bank balances
- real private messages
- real medical info
- addresses, IDs, license plates
- salary or confidential work data
- real private documents
- dangerous or unsafe situations

Private-data-adjacent ideas are acceptable only if clearly framed as fake/demo/cropped/mock screens.
