Next step: PART 4 — Swipe gestures + animations

Goal:
Turn the static feed into a smooth, native-feeling swipe experience.

Focus ONLY on interaction and motion.
Do not add backend, reactions persistence, or nav changes yet.

---

SCOPE

Add swipe gestures to SwipeIdeaCard:

- Swipe UP → next idea
- Swipe RIGHT → save (visual only, no backend yet)
- Swipe LEFT → skip

---

IMPLEMENTATION

Use:
- react-native-gesture-handler
- react-native-reanimated

(Do not introduce new libraries beyond these)

---

BEHAVIOR

1. Swipe UP (primary)
- vertical swipe
- threshold-based trigger
- card animates up and fades out
- next idea slides in from bottom
- preload next idea (already available in array)

2. Swipe RIGHT (save)
- card moves right + slight rotation
- quick snap animation
- then return to center (since no persistence yet)
- optional small “Saved” indicator flash

3. Swipe LEFT (skip)
- card moves left
- fades slightly
- returns to center

---

ANIMATION RULES

- duration: ~200–300ms
- easing: smooth, slightly springy (not stiff)
- no jank
- 60fps target

---

STATE MANAGEMENT

- keep current index in feed.tsx
- pass idea + index to SwipeIdeaCard
- update index on swipe up only

---

VISUAL FEEDBACK

While dragging:
- card follows finger
- slight rotation based on horizontal drag
- opacity reduces slightly on strong swipe

---

CONSTRAINTS

Do NOT:
- implement reaction logic yet
- persist saves/skips
- change data fetching
- modify bottom nav
- change card layout

---

SUCCESS CRITERIA

- feels like TikTok swipe (smooth, responsive)
- no lag or flicker
- user can quickly swipe through ideas
- swipe direction is intuitive
- animation feels satisfying

---

QA

Test:
- rapid swiping
- edge swipes
- small vs large gestures
- no crashes
- no stuck states

---

IMPORTANT

This step is about FEEL, not features.

If the motion feels off, we fix it before adding anything else.
