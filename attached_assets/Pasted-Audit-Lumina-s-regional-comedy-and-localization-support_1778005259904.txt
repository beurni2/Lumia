I want you to understand this product requirement clearly:

Lumina should not generate the exact same hooks, scenarios, captions, and filming ideas for every region.

The long-term vision is that Lumina understands that humor, language style, daily life, and creator culture differ by region.

For example:
- A creator in Nigeria may want ideas that can use Nigerian Pidgin, Nigerian social dynamics, WhatsApp/group chat behavior, transport stress, family/auntie/uncle dynamics, NEPA/light/data realities, local food/home situations, and day-to-day Nigerian creator life.
- A creator in India may want ideas that can use Hinglish, Indian family/social dynamics, college/work pressure, commute/traffic/train realities, group chat culture, exams/study pressure, food delivery, hostel/roommate life, and day-to-day Indian creator life.
- A creator in the US, UK, Ghana, South Africa, Philippines, etc. may each need different language rhythms, local references, daily-life anchors, and comedy expectations.

This is not just translation.

The goal is not:
“Take the same English hook and translate it.”

The goal is:
“Generate ideas that feel culturally natural, locally relevant, and creator-native for that user’s region and language style.”

Lumina’s core promise should remain:
The user gets funny, personal, filmable ideas they would actually post.

But region should eventually influence:
- hook wording
- scenario details
- caption phrasing
- daily-life anchors
- situation/topic weighting
- language style
- slang intensity
- cultural context
- filming reality
- what feels relatable or cringe in that region

Important:
Do not assume every user from a country wants the local dialect or slang.
The app should eventually distinguish between:
- region
- language style
- slang/local-language intensity
- creator preference

For example:
A Nigerian creator may want:
- clean English
- English + light Pidgin
- full Nigerian Pidgin style

An Indian creator may want:
- clean English
- English + light Hinglish
- stronger Hinglish style

A user’s selected region should not force one language style automatically.

The product should avoid fake, awkward, or stereotyped localization.
Bad regional comedy is worse than no regional comedy.

If Lumina is not confident in a regional wording style, it should prefer clean natural English with locally relevant scenario details rather than awkward slang.
